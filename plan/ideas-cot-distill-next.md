## Stage 2 — CANDIDATES (5-field MechanismHypothesis)

### C1: Closure Inheritance Test (Round 6A — the decisive experiment)

```yaml
silent_axiom: "A model's reasoning closure is determined by architecture and
  optimizer, not by the closure quality of its training text"
mechanism: Same-base controlled comparison — generate <think> traces from
  Jackrong/Qwen3.5-9B-GLM5.1-Distill-v1 (trained on high-closure GLM data,
  CCR 1.67) and Jackrong/Qwen3.5-9B-DeepSeek-V4-Flash (trained on low-closure
  DeepSeek data, CCR 0.60) on the SAME 60 matched problems from the natural
  experiment; judge behavioral CCR with the same Opus rubric
hypothesis: "A student trained on high-closure data produces reasoning with
  measurably higher behavioral closure than a student trained on low-closure
  data, holding base model (Qwen3.5-9B) and scale constant"
observable: Behavioral CCR of GLM-student > DeepSeek-student; Wilcoxon p < 0.01,
  Cliff's δ > 0.3; behavioral ranking reproduces training-data ranking
falsifier: If Wilcoxon p > 0.1 AND |Cliff's δ| < 0.1 → text closure does NOT
  transmit to behavior; the entire audit is descriptive, not predictive. The
  CCR filter loses its rationale.
```
**Controls:** negative = base Qwen3.5-9B (no fine-tuning); equal-compute = both
models are same-scale same-architecture SFT'd for comparable steps; holdout = the
60 matched problems are from the natural experiment, not from either training set.

### C2: Closure → Benchmark Correlation (Round 7 — the practical punchline)

```yaml
silent_axiom: "Training-data closure is orthogonal to downstream reasoning
  accuracy — models learn to reason correctly regardless of whether training
  text shows the self-correction process"
mechanism: Cross-model benchmark scoring — run GSM8K / MATH-500 / a reasoning
  suite on ≥4 Jackrong models with known training-data closure (GLM-student,
  DeepSeek-student, Qwopus3.5-9B, Qwopus3.6-27B), then correlate
hypothesis: "Models trained on higher-closure data achieve higher accuracy on
  held-out reasoning benchmarks, after controlling for model scale"
observable: Spearman ρ(training-data CCR, benchmark accuracy) > 0.7 across ≥4
  models at the same scale; or ρ > 0.5 across all available models
falsifier: If |ρ| < 0.3 → closure quality does not predict reasoning ability;
  "better-looking" reasoning (high closure) ≠ "better" reasoning. Training on
  raw/messy traces works just as well.
```
**Controls:** negative = base Qwen3.5-9B (no SFT); equal-compute = same base
model family; holdout = standard benchmarks never seen in training.

### C3: Extended CCR Audit — New Teacher Families

```yaml
silent_axiom: "The 10 datasets already audited are representative; new teacher
  families won't change the vocabulary-vs-closure picture"
mechanism: Apply the existing CCR pipeline (Opus judge + RAE/RT structural) to
  Jackrong's NEW datasets: (a) GPT-OSS-120B-Distilled-Reasoning-math (new teacher),
  (b) glm-4.7-Superior-Reasoning-stage1 (new GLM version), (c) Chinese-Qwen3-235B-
  Thinking-2507-Distill-100k (new teacher + cross-lingual), (d) LogosForge-scored-
  sft-v1 (external quality labels)
hypothesis: "New teacher families (GPT-OSS, Qwen3-235B, GLM-4.7) occupy distinct
  CCR positions AND the vocabulary-closure dissociation generalizes; LogosForge
  quality labels correlate with CCR (cross-validation of metrics)"
observable: ≥2 new datasets show vocabulary-closure dissociation; cross-dataset
  RAE ranking extends with Spearman ≥ 0.8; LogosForge score–CCR Spearman ≥ 0.5
falsifier: If ALL new datasets cluster at similar CCR AND no vocabulary-closure
  dissociation appears → the phenomenon is GLM/DeepSeek-specific, not general
```
**Controls:** negative = datasets already known to have low closure (nohurry, roman)
are included as anchors; holdout = the new datasets were never part of the original
pipeline development.

### C4: CCR-Guided Data Selection at Scale

```yaml
silent_axiom: "All training traces from a given teacher are equally valuable;
  per-trace CCR filtering doesn't improve subset quality compared to random"
mechanism: Large-scale subsample audit — draw 1000 traces from GLM-5.1-Reasoning-
  1M (527k total), run CCR judging, partition into CCR≥2 and CCR<2, compare
  structural profiles (RAE, RT, MI, length, topology distribution, overthrow%)
hypothesis: "CCR≥2 filtered subsets have higher RAE (>0.3 bits above full-set
  mean), more non-linear topology, and maintain practical yield (>20% retention)"
observable: CCR≥2 subset RAE > full-set RAE + 0.3; CCR≥2 retention rate 20-60%;
  structural metrics of CCR≥2 subset match the top-3 datasets from the audit
falsifier: If CCR filtering retains <5% of traces (impractical) OR ΔRAe < 0.1
  (no structural improvement) → per-trace filtering is too costly or uninformative
```
**Controls:** negative = random subsample of same size as CCR≥2 set;
equal-compute = same judge cost per trace.

### C5: GSPO vs SFT Behavioral Closure Comparison

```yaml
silent_axiom: "The training method (SFT vs GSPO/RL) doesn't affect the model's
  self-correction behavior — only the training data does"
mechanism: Generate <think> traces from Qwopus3.5-27B (SFT-only) and
  Qwopus3.6-27B (GSPO, RL-enhanced) on the same problem set, judge with CCR rubric
hypothesis: "GSPO-trained models produce higher behavioral closure than SFT-only
  models, even when trained on similar or overlapping data — RL teaches the
  correction ACT, not just the vocabulary, which SFT alone cannot"
observable: Qwopus3.6-27B CCR > Qwopus3.5-27B CCR; Wilcoxon p < 0.05,
  Cliff's δ > 0.2; specifically, overthrow% should be higher for GSPO
falsifier: If Qwopus3.6 ≤ Qwopus3.5 on closure → GSPO doesn't add closure
  beyond what SFT provides; only the data quality matters (reinforcing C1/C2).
  This would mean our own SFT distillation should focus exclusively on data
  selection, not training method.
```
**Controls:** negative = base Qwen3.5 (no SFT); note confound: these are
different model versions (3.5 vs 3.6), not a clean ablation of training method.
The comparison is observational, not causal.

### C6: Cross-Lingual Closure Generalization

```yaml
silent_axiom: "The vocabulary-vs-closure dissociation is language-specific
  (English) and may not hold in Chinese reasoning text"
mechanism: Audit Chinese-Qwen3-235B-Thinking-2507-Distill-100k and Chinese-
  DeepSeek-V3.2-Exp-chat-example with the CCR rubric (using Chinese-capable
  judges), then compare closure distributions with English equivalents
hypothesis: "The vocabulary-closure dissociation is language-invariant — Chinese
  reasoning text shows the same pattern (high density ≠ high closure), and the
  RAE structural metric generalizes to Chinese text"
observable: Chinese datasets show vocabulary-closure dissociation; RAE computed
  on Chinese text ranks datasets consistently with LLM-judged CCR (Spearman ≥ 0.6)
falsifier: If Chinese datasets show strong vocabulary-closure alignment (high
  density = high closure) → the dissociation is an English-specific artifact of
  how critique markers work in English. This would mean CCR-based filtering needs
  language-specific calibration.
```
**Controls:** negative = English datasets at matched length; the judge prompt
must be adapted for Chinese critique markers ("不对/等等/但是/重新").

### C7: LogosForge Cross-Validation of CCR

```yaml
silent_axiom: "Our CCR metric measures something unique and can't be validated
  against external quality labels"
mechanism: LogosForge-scored-sft-v1 has external quality scores for each trace.
  Score a subsample with our CCR rubric, correlate with LogosForge labels
hypothesis: "CCR captures a dimension of quality that partially overlaps with but
  is not redundant to external scoring — Spearman 0.3-0.7 (convergent validity
  without perfect redundancy)"
observable: CCR × LogosForge-score Spearman ∈ [0.3, 0.7]; CCR captures cases
  LogosForge misses (high LogosForge but low CCR = surface quality without closure)
falsifier: If Spearman < 0.1 → CCR and external scoring are unrelated, raising
  questions about what CCR actually measures. If Spearman > 0.9 → CCR is redundant
  with simpler scoring.
```
**Controls:** the correlation direction matters — we expect partial overlap, not
identity or orthogonality.
